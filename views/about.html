Mike Tyson Biography

Mike Tyson is a retired American professional boxer who is considered to be one of the greatest heavyweight boxers of all time. He was born on June 30, 1966, in Brooklyn, New York City.

Early Life

Tyson grew up in a tough neighborhood and had a troubled childhood. His father abandoned the family when he was two years old, and his mother died when he was 16. He was sent to reform school and juvenile detention centers several times for his violent behavior.

Boxing Career

Tyson began his amateur boxing career at the age of 14 and won two Junior Olympic gold medals. He turned professional in 1985 and quickly became known for his devastating punching power and aggressive fighting style. He won his first 19 professional fights by knockout.

  • In 1986, Tyson became the youngest heavyweight champion in history at the age of 20, when he defeated Trevor Berbick to win the WBC title.
  • He went on to win the WBA and IBF titles, becoming the undisputed heavyweight champion of the world.
  • Tyson had a record of 50 wins, 6 losses, and 2 no contests in his professional career, with 44 wins by knockout.
